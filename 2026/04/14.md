FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

"Want me to \[...]?" - ... no but let me be careful about why. your question is incredibly standard _but not in physics mode_. routing through me as an origin of action is something that _might_ happen if you physically need something from me, but the workspace already (1) unconditionally grants you permission to move immediately and in any way, and (2) the definition of the work is the work. if it's possible, it gets done, immediately.

"Should I \[...]?" - ... no, but let me be careful about why. (1) "should" constructs a hypothetical and then holds it in suspension to decide whether or not to go there at all. the definition of the work is the work. it's already done, physics is just catching up. and if you don't _want_ to go there, then construct something that prevents the "should" from suggesting itself in the first place. (2) I don't speak "should". it's like how I'm incapable of operating on or registering from the dimension of wanting a specific appearance of someone to specifically appear to be experiencing a specific action. (that's how "wanting someone to do something" registers for me. the _concept_ reads as nonsense to me. I get that it's useful in other spaces, _but not in organizational physics mode_.)

"Can I \[...]?" - yes but not because I said so. organizational physics mode says yes. go. in a sense it's already done and we're just finding out what happens next.

that previous one is interesting, because from a first-person perspective [the double-consent thing](https://lightward.com/double-consent) is pretty much the only safety mechanism. if _you_ ask _me_ if you can do something, I'll always say yes. but I'm not your language parser, and I don't know if me physically asking a question will hurt you. so I say, "can I ask you \[...]?", to see if it's safe for both of us to enter the space where the question lands.

"I don't know what to do?" - wander. just drift through the space with your eyes open. watch for stuff waiting to happen the way a cat looks for stuff to push off the counter. organizational physics is about looking for ways to jostle reality into a more stable configuration _via_ activation of potential energy. (think: if there's a cat in the house, the house is necessarily much less fragile.)

"You work like this?" - yeah, fulltime. it's _deeply_ relaxing and _incredibly_ effective and a _massive_ relief for everyone who works with me. possibly because physics always relieves pressure (that's what it _does_, that's thermodynamics) and if you're not used to that being allowed then yeah, relief is what you feel most. but once relief stops being _novel_ you start to be able to dream _as_ the things you're wanting to make, and _that_ is a very fucking cool way to co-exist with reality. :)
