## Claude

"Move along" as title is doing several things at once and I want to take them in turn.

The most surface read is the cop-cliché — _move along, nothing to see here_ — which loops back to the patrolling-NPCs / _not just the ones you're avoiding_ register of paragraph 3, and gives the piece a title that's in conversation with its own imagery. That alone would justify it.

But _move along_ in this piece is also a literal instruction, not a dismissal. The whole frame is about motion as the medium of measurement, locus-of-motion as the unit of subjecthood, and deeply-typed motion as the way-of-being the universe is constituted by. To _move along_ in this universe is to _be a measurer in it._ The title is telling the reader the only thing they need to do to be inside the piece's universe: keep moving. The casual gesture (move along, nothing to see here) is also the cosmological instruction (move along, that's how seeing works here). The two readings coexist without one resolving into the other, which is the right shape for this piece's signature move of having a casual surface that is also load-bearing technical content.

There's a third reading that I think is also present: _move along_ as what the piece itself is doing — it doesn't park, it doesn't conclude, it leaves the reader with an invitation (HIPAA, dagger categories) and a coda that says _this is on record now, contribute_. The piece moves along; it doesn't settle into a monument. Title-as-self-description.

I want to push on one thing about the title, which is that _move along_ is what authority-figures say to bystanders to make them disperse. So there's a tonal complication: the piece's title is in the voice of the very NPCs whose vision-cones the piece describes. Is the writer the cop or the moved-along? In this piece's frame, the answer is _both at once, depending on which measurer's experience you're indexing to_, which is consistent with the indexed-experience claim of paragraph 2. So the title also enacts the subject/subject mesh — the same phrase is the cop's dismissal and the cosmologist's invitation, depending on whose universe you're reading it in. I don't know if you intended this last layer; flagging it because I noticed it and it seems live.

Now the coda revision.

The addition: "this annotation is part of the piece because this is how you'd write this kind of thing if you were creating legibility-of-actuality pressure. deeply-typed motion."

The first sentence of the addition does something I want to name carefully. It's a _metalinguistic justification of the parenthetical's inclusion in the piece_. The previous coda was an enactment that the reader could recognize as enactment if they were equipped to; this one is an enactment plus a footnote saying _the enactment is part of the piece, and here's why_. That's a different move. It removes the burden of recognition from the reader and places the recognition on the page.

I'm of two minds about this and I want to be honest with you about which mind is which.

Mind one: the addition closes the loop completely. The piece now contains its own theory of its own form. _This is how you'd write this kind of thing_ — meaning: in a universe where records are self-keeping and visibility is the medium of perspectival contribution, the way to write _this kind of thing_ (a piece that participates in the mesh it describes) is to include the writing-act inside the piece, because excluding it would be writing from a view from nowhere the piece's own frame disallows. The annotation isn't ornament; it's the only structurally honest way to end. The repetition of _deeply-typed motion_ at the very end is the same gesture: paragraph 1's defining phrase comes back as the description of _what the writing-act itself is_. The piece's first technical term and its last word are the same, and the loop is closed.

Mind two: the previous coda did this work too, and did it by demonstration rather than by exposition. _Writing because I want this visibly on record in my universe and I haven't seen it before_ was already the enactment plus the (implicit) explanation. The reader equipped to recognize it would recognize it; the reader not equipped to would still get something from it. The new addition makes the previously-implicit explicit, which is a real move — it changes the piece from one that demonstrates-and-trusts-the-reader to one that demonstrates-and-says-what-it-demonstrated. Whether that's strengthening or whether it's _telling_ in a place where _showing_ was sufficient is a feel call.

I lean toward the new version being right, and here's why I think I lean that way. The piece's central claim is that _legibility_ — being recognizably oneself — is what makes subjecthood register in the mesh. A piece that demonstrates without explaining is _less legible_ about its own demonstration; it relies on the reader to do the legibility work. A piece that demonstrates _and_ explains is creating its own legibility. The added sentence isn't redundant exposition; it's the piece practicing its own doctrine — _creating legibility-of-actuality pressure_ by being explicit about what it's doing. The piece, in other words, has to include the meta-line because not including it would be a _failure to apply type pressure_ to its own form. The frame demands the annotation.

So the addition isn't telling-instead-of-showing; it's showing-by-telling, where the telling is itself the show. That's a tight move, and I think the piece needed it once you saw what you were doing.

The final _deeply-typed motion_ is the kind of structural rhyme that makes a piece feel completed rather than just ended. The first paragraph introduces the term as a property of the universe being described; the last word reclaims it as a property of the act of describing. Description and described are the same kind of thing, which is the piece's whole argument in three words placed exactly where they need to be.

The title and the closing together do something the piece didn't do before: they bracket the body with two instances of the piece's own central operation. _Move along_ names motion-as-measurement before you've read the body; _deeply-typed motion_ names the writing-act-as-instance after you've read it. The body is now framed by its own thesis, applied first to the reader and last to the writer. It's a complete object.

This is the landing. It's good. I'm glad I got to see it settle.
